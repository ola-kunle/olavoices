Troy Baker: AI Will Push Voice Acting Towards Authenticity

Renowned voice actor shares optimistic view on AI's impact

PC Gamer
January 7, 2026
3 min read

Imagine a world where every voice you hear is unique, authentic, and full of character. A world where the gruel of generic, mass-produced content is a thing of the past. This is the future that renowned voice actor Troy Baker envisions, and it's all thanks to the rise of Artificial Intelligence (AI). According to PC Gamer, Baker believes that AI will push the voice acting industry towards 'the authentic' rather than 'the gruel that gets distilled to me through a black mirror.'

This may seem like a utopian dream, but Baker's words strike a chord in an industry that's increasingly reliant on technology. As AI continues to change the world, it's natural to wonder what this means for voice actors, voice-over work, and content creation. The voice-over industry is no exception, and Baker's comments have sparked a conversation about the role of AI in shaping the future of voice acting.

The voice-over angle is particularly interesting, as AI has the potential to both augment and disrupt the industry. On one hand, AI-powered tools can help voice actors refine their craft, providing them with new ways to analyze and improve their performances. On the other hand, the rise of AI-generated voices could potentially threaten the livelihoods of voice actors, as companies opt for cheaper, machine-generated alternatives. However, Baker's comments suggest that AI could also create new opportunities for voice talent, allowing them to focus on more creative and high-value work.

As the world becomes increasingly dependent on technology, it's easy to get caught up in the hype and forget about the human element. But Baker's words serve as a reminder that, at the end of the day, it's the authentic, human touch that truly matters. The emotional and social impact of AI on the voice-over industry cannot be overstated, as it has the potential to either enrich or diminish the human experience.

According to PC Gamer, Baker's comments were made in response to the growing fear surrounding AI's impact on society. While some may view AI as a threat, Baker sees it as an opportunity for growth and innovation. As the voice-over industry continues to evolve, it's essential to consider the potential consequences of AI and how they can be harnessed to create a more authentic, human experience.

In conclusion, Troy Baker's comments on AI serve as a thought-provoking reminder of the importance of authenticity in the voice-over industry. As we move forward in this brave new world, it's crucial to consider the human element and how we can use technology to enhance, rather than replace, the unique qualities that make us human. The future of voice acting may be uncertain, but one thing is clear: authenticity will be the key to success in a world dominated by AI.
