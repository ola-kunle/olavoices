Voice Actors in Videogames: The Unseen Struggle

Studios and fans disconnected from voice actor realities

PC Gamer
December 1, 2025
3 min read

Imagine bringing a character to life with your voice, only to be seen as an outsider in the very industry you're helping to thrive. This is the harsh reality for many voice actors in the videogame industry, including Samantha Béart, the talented voice behind Karlach in Baldur's Gate 3. According to PC Gamer, Béart recently expressed her concerns about the disconnect between videogame studios and voice actors, stating that 'we're still seen as outsources' in the game development process.

The videogame industry has experienced tremendous growth over the years, with many titles relying heavily on voice acting to create immersive experiences for players. However, the relationship between voice actors and videogame studios has been strained, with the looming threat of AI-generated voice acting casting a shadow over the industry. As Béart pointed out, there's a 'weird disconnect' between the realities of studios and fans, with voice actors often being viewed as external contractors rather than integral members of the development team.

This perception has significant implications for the voice-over industry as a whole. Voice actors are not just providing a service; they're bringing characters to life, conveying emotions, and creating connections with players. The fact that they're still seen as 'outsources' suggests a lack of understanding about the value they bring to the table. As the videogame industry continues to evolve, it's essential to recognize the importance of voice actors and the challenges they face. The rise of AI-generated voice acting, for instance, poses a significant threat to the livelihoods of voice actors, who may find themselves replaced by automated systems.

The voice-over angle is particularly significant in this context. Voice actors are not just limited to the videogame industry; they work across various mediums, including film, television, and animation. The devaluation of voice actors in the videogame industry can have far-reaching consequences, affecting the entire voice-over industry. It's crucial for voice-over professionals to care about this issue, as it can impact their own work and the value they're perceived to bring to their clients. Moreover, the opportunities for voice talent in the videogame industry are vast, with many studios recognizing the importance of high-quality voice acting in creating engaging experiences. However, this requires a shift in perception, with voice actors being seen as integral members of the development team rather than external contractors.

The emotional and social impact of this issue cannot be overstated. Voice actors are not just providing a service; they're creating characters that resonate with players, evoking emotions, and fostering connections. The disconnect between studios and voice actors can have a profound effect on the final product, with players being able to sense when a character's voice doesn't quite feel authentic. Furthermore, the lack of recognition for voice actors can perpetuate a culture of undervaluation, where their contributions are seen as secondary to other aspects of game development.

In conclusion, the struggle of voice actors in the videogame industry is a pressing concern that requires attention and understanding. As the industry continues to evolve, it's essential to recognize the value that voice actors bring to the table and to work towards creating a more inclusive and collaborative environment. By doing so, we can ensure that the characters we love are brought to life with authenticity and passion, and that the voice actors who breathe life into them are valued and respected for their craft. As Béart so eloquently put it, it's time to address the 'weird disconnect' and work towards a more harmonious relationship between studios, fans, and voice actors.
