Charlie Cox Shines Light on Voice Acting Credit

Nominations for Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 spark industry discussion

Wccftech
November 25, 2025
3 min read

Imagine being part of a groundbreaking project, pouring your heart and soul into it, and then watching as someone else takes the credit. This is the reality that British actor Charlie Cox is speaking out against, as he addresses the nominations for his work on the game Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 at Geoff Keighley's The Game Awards. According to Wccftech, Cox believes that the nominations should be credited to the motion actor, rather than himself.

Cox, a renowned film and TV actor with credits including Stardust, Boardwalk Empire, and The Theory of Everything, has lent his voice to numerous projects over the years. His work on Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 is just one example of the incredible talent that goes into bringing games to life. The Game Awards, which took place on December 12, recognize excellence in the gaming industry, and Cox's nomination is a testament to the dedication and craftsmanship that he and his team brought to the project.

The voice-over industry is a crucial part of the gaming world, with talented actors like Cox bringing characters to life and immersing players in new worlds. However, the issue of credit and recognition is one that affects voice actors and voice-over professionals deeply. As the industry continues to evolve, it's essential that we acknowledge the hard work and dedication that goes into creating these experiences. The voice-over angle is particularly significant in this story, as it highlights the often-overlooked contributions of voice actors to the gaming industry. Voice-over professionals should care about this story because it speaks to the importance of proper crediting and recognition for their work, which can have a significant impact on their careers and the industry as a whole.

The impact of this story extends beyond the gaming industry, speaking to broader issues of credit and recognition in the entertainment industry. It's a reminder that the people behind the scenes, from motion actors to voice-over artists, are the ones who bring our favorite characters and stories to life. As we celebrate the achievements of actors like Cox, we must also acknowledge the talented individuals who work tirelessly behind the scenes to create these experiences. This story matters because it sheds light on the often-invisible work of voice-over professionals and the importance of giving credit where credit is due.

In conclusion, Charlie Cox's comments on the nominations for Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 are a powerful reminder of the importance of recognizing and crediting the talented individuals who bring our favorite games and stories to life. As we look to the future of the voice-over industry, it's essential that we prioritize proper crediting and recognition, ensuring that the hard work and dedication of voice-over professionals are valued and respected. By doing so, we can create a more inclusive and equitable industry that celebrates the talented individuals who make it all possible.
