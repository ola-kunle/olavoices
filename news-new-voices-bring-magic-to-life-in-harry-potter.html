New Voices Bring Magic to Life in Harry Potter

Iwan Rheon and Blake Harrison debut in Audible's audiobooks

Variety
January 13, 2026
3 min read

Imagine stepping into the wizarding world of Harry Potter, where the sounds and voices transport you to a magical realm. For fans of the beloved series, the experience just got a whole lot more enchanting. According to Variety, Iwan Rheon and Blake Harrison are the new Moony and Wormtail in Audible's 'Harry Potter' audiobooks, bringing their unique talents to the roles of Prof. Remus Lupin and Peter Pettigrew in 'Prisoner of Azkaban'.

The duo's debut marks an exciting milestone for the franchise, which has been captivating audiences for decades. Iwan Rheon, known for his role in 'Game of Thrones', and Blake Harrison, famous for 'The Inbetweeners', are sure to bring a fresh energy to the characters. Their performances will undoubtedly delight listeners, from the haunting howl of Lupin's werewolf transformation to the sly, high-pitched voice of Pettigrew's mouse disguise.

For voice-over professionals, this news is particularly significant. The Harry Potter series is a cultural phenomenon, and being part of its audiobook production is a dream come true for many voice actors. The fact that Audible is continuously investing in high-quality voice talent underscores the importance of voice-over work in content creation. As the demand for audiobooks and immersive audio experiences continues to grow, voice actors are presented with new opportunities to showcase their skills and bring beloved characters to life. However, this also means that the competition is fiercer than ever, and voice talent must be prepared to adapt to the evolving landscape of the industry.

The impact of this news extends beyond the voice-over community, as it highlights the power of storytelling and its ability to evoke emotions, spark imagination, and foster connections. The Harry Potter series has become a cultural touchstone, transcending generations and geographical boundaries. By introducing new voices to the franchise, Audible is ensuring that the magic of the wizarding world remains accessible and engaging for a diverse range of listeners. As fans embark on this new audio journey, they will be reminded of the transformative power of storytelling and the importance of empathy, friendship, and perseverance.

In conclusion, the debut of Iwan Rheon and Blake Harrison in Audible's 'Harry Potter' audiobooks is a testament to the enduring appeal of the series and the significance of voice-over work in bringing stories to life. As the voice-over industry continues to evolve, it is exciting to think about the new opportunities and challenges that lie ahead for voice actors, and how they will shape the future of storytelling. According to Variety, the release of 'Prisoner of Azkaban' marks the beginning of a new chapter in the Harry Potter audiobook series, and we can't wait to see what's in store for listeners.
