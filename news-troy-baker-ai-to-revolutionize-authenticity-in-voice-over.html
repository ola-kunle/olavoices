Troy Baker: AI to Revolutionize Authenticity in Voice-Over

Voice actor weighs in on AI's potential impact

PC Gamer
January 7, 2026
3 min read

Imagine a world where every voice you hear is authentic, unique, and uncompromising. A world where the gruel of mass-produced content is replaced by genuine, heartfelt expression. According to PC Gamer, renowned voice actor Troy Baker believes that's exactly what AI will bring to the table. In a recent interview, Baker expressed his enthusiasm for the technology, stating that it will push us toward 'the authentic' rather than 'the gruel that gets distilled to me through a black mirror.'

This statement may seem overly optimistic to some, but it highlights the potential for AI to revolutionize the way we approach content creation. As AI technology continues to advance, it's likely that we'll see significant changes in the voice-over industry. Voice actors, in particular, will need to adapt to a new landscape where AI-generated voices are increasingly prevalent.

The voice-over industry is poised to be significantly impacted by AI, with potential applications ranging from automated dialogue replacement to the creation of entirely new characters. According to PC Gamer, Baker's comments suggest that he believes AI will help to elevate the art of voice acting, rather than replace it. This could lead to new opportunities for voice-over professionals, as they focus on bringing authentic, nuanced performances to the forefront.

However, this shift also raises important questions about the role of human voice actors in a world where AI can generate convincing voices. Will AI replace human voice actors, or will it augment their abilities? How will the industry balance the need for authentic, human performances with the efficiency and cost-effectiveness of AI-generated content?

As we consider the potential implications of AI on the voice-over industry, it's essential to remember that the human touch is what sets great voice acting apart. The ability to convey emotion, to bring depth and nuance to a character, is a uniquely human trait. While AI may be able to mimic certain aspects of human speech, it lacks the heart and soul that a skilled voice actor brings to a role.

Ultimately, the integration of AI into the voice-over industry will require a delicate balance between technology and human creativity. As Troy Baker so eloquently put it, the goal should be to push toward 'the authentic,' rather than settling for 'the gruel' of mass-produced content. By embracing this shift and focusing on what makes human voice acting special, we can create a brighter future for the voice-over industry, one that is filled with authenticity, emotion, and heart.
