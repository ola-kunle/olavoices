Podcasting Duo Opens Up About Breakdown

Communication struggles and friendship tested

Independent.ie
January 10, 2026
3 min read

Imagine meeting your future business partner on a boozy flight, and seven years later, you're still going strong, having landed A-list guests like Paul Mescal on your podcast. For Kevin Twomey and PJ Kirby, the Cork podcasting duo behind 'I'm Grand Mam', this is their reality. However, as they recently revealed, their journey hasn't been without its challenges. According to Independent.ie, the pair have faced communication breakdowns that threatened to derail not just their professional partnership, but their friendship as well.

The duo's story is one of instant connection, with their podcast born out of a chance meeting on a flight from Budapest nearly seven years ago. Since then, they've experienced several 'pinch-me' career moments, including landing high-profile guests and building a loyal listener base. Their success is a testament to the power of podcasting, which has become an increasingly popular medium for storytelling and self-expression. As voice-over professionals, the story of Kevin Twomey and PJ Kirby serves as a reminder of the importance of effective communication and collaboration in content creation.

The voice-over industry is built on the back of strong relationships between talent, directors, and producers. A breakdown in communication can have serious consequences, from delayed projects to lost opportunities. The story of 'I'm Grand Mam' highlights the need for voice-over professionals to prioritize communication and collaboration, not just with their colleagues, but with their audience as well. By doing so, they can build trust, foster creativity, and produce high-quality content that resonates with listeners.

The impact of Kevin Twomey and PJ Kirby's story extends beyond the voice-over industry, speaking to the universal human experience of navigating relationships and overcoming challenges. Their willingness to open up about their struggles serves as a powerful reminder that even in the darkest moments, there is always hope for redemption and growth. As we reflect on their journey, we're reminded that the true power of podcasting and voice-over work lies not just in the content itself, but in the connections we make along the way.

In conclusion, the story of Kevin Twomey and PJ Kirby is a powerful reminder of the importance of communication, collaboration, and resilience in the voice-over industry. As we strive to create content that inspires and connects with our audience, we must prioritize the relationships that make it all possible. By doing so, we can build a stronger, more supportive community that celebrates the art of voice-over and the power of the human voice.
