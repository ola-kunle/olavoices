Deus Ex Voice Actor Unleashes Scathing Critique

Elias Toufexis calls out Embracer Group over game development

Wccftech
January 2, 2026
3 min read

Imagine being the voice behind a beloved character, only to see the franchise you've poured your heart into stalled indefinitely. This is the harsh reality facing Elias Toufexis, the talented voice actor behind Adam Jensen in the Deus Ex series. According to Wccftech, Toufexis recently expressed his frustration with the Embracer Group, the company in charge of the Deus Ex franchise, stating that 'the people in charge are psychopaths' for not greenlighting a new game in 2026.

The Deus Ex series has been a staple of the gaming world for decades, with its thought-provoking themes and immersive gameplay. As the voice of Adam Jensen, Toufexis has been an integral part of the series' success. However, with the lack of a new game on the horizon, fans and voice actors alike are left wondering what the future holds. The Embracer Group's decision not to move forward with a new Deus Ex game has significant implications for the gaming industry, but it also raises important questions about the role of voice actors in game development.

As a voice actor, Toufexis's comments highlight the often-overlooked relationship between voice talent and game developers. Voice actors bring characters to life, investing their time and energy into making the gaming experience more engaging and believable. However, they often have limited control over the development process and may be left in the dark about the future of a franchise. This can lead to uncertainty and frustration, as seen in Toufexis's comments. The voice-over industry is a critical component of game development, with talented voice actors like Toufexis bringing depth and emotion to characters. The lack of a new Deus Ex game not only affects the gaming community but also has a ripple effect on the voice-over industry, potentially impacting the livelihoods of voice actors and the quality of future games.

The impact of the Embracer Group's decision extends beyond the gaming world, touching on issues of creativity, ownership, and the value of intellectual property. When a company decides not to move forward with a beloved franchise, it can be devastating for fans and the people involved in its creation. This situation raises important questions about the role of corporations in the creative process and the importance of considering the human element in decision-making. As the gaming industry continues to evolve, it's essential to recognize the value of voice actors and their contributions to the gaming experience.

In conclusion, Elias Toufexis's comments serve as a reminder of the complex and often fraught relationship between voice actors, game developers, and the corporations that own the rights to beloved franchises. As the voice-over industry continues to grow and evolve, it's essential to prioritize the needs and concerns of voice actors, recognizing the critical role they play in bringing characters to life. The future of the Deus Ex franchise may be uncertain, but one thing is clear: the voice-over industry will continue to be a vital part of the gaming experience, and it's time to start listening to the voices behind the characters we love.
