Magical Voices Bring Harry Potter to Life

Iwan Rheon and Blake Harrison debut in Audiobooks

Variety
January 13, 2026
3 min read

Imagine stepping into the wizarding world of Harry Potter, where the characters come alive through the magic of voice acting. For fans of the beloved series, the experience just got a whole lot more enchanting. According to Variety, Iwan Rheon and Blake Harrison are the new voices behind Prof. Remus Lupin and Peter Pettigrew in Audible's 'Harry Potter' audiobooks, making their debuts in 'Prisoner of Azkaban'.

The news has sent shockwaves of excitement through the fan community, with many eager to hear the actors' interpretations of the iconic characters. Iwan Rheon, known for his role in 'Game of Thrones', and Blake Harrison, from 'The Inbetweeners', bring their unique talents to the table, breathing new life into the stories. As they delve into the world of Hogwarts, they face the challenge of creating distinct voices for the characters, including Lupin's werewolf howl and Pettigrew's mouse voice.

The process of finding the perfect voice for a character is a painstaking one, requiring a deep understanding of the story and its characters. For voice actors, this is an opportunity to showcase their range and versatility, bringing a new layer of depth to the narrative. The voice-over industry is a vital part of the storytelling process, with talented actors like Iwan Rheon and Blake Harrison pushing the boundaries of what is possible. As the demand for high-quality audiobooks continues to grow, voice actors are in high demand, and this latest development is a testament to the power of voice acting in bringing stories to life.

The impact of this news on the voice-over industry cannot be overstated. With the rise of audiobooks and podcasting, voice actors are no longer just limited to traditional voice-over work. They are now an integral part of the storytelling process, with the ability to shape the narrative and bring characters to life in a way that was previously unimaginable. For voice-over professionals, this creates a wealth of opportunities, from working on high-profile projects like the 'Harry Potter' series to experimenting with new formats and genres. However, it also presents challenges, such as the need to constantly adapt to new technologies and formats, and to stay ahead of the curve in terms of industry trends.

So why does this matter? For fans of the 'Harry Potter' series, it's a chance to experience the stories in a new and exciting way. For voice actors, it's an opportunity to showcase their talents and push the boundaries of what is possible. But on a deeper level, it's about the power of storytelling to bring people together and to transport us to new and imaginative worlds. As we listen to Iwan Rheon and Blake Harrison bring Lupin and Pettigrew to life, we are reminded of the magic of voice acting and the impact it can have on our lives.

In conclusion, the debut of Iwan Rheon and Blake Harrison in the 'Harry Potter' audiobooks is a testament to the power of voice acting and the impact it can have on our lives. As we look to the future of the voice-over industry, it's clear that the possibilities are endless, and that talented actors like Iwan Rheon and Blake Harrison will continue to push the boundaries of what is possible. So let's raise a wand to the magic of voice acting, and to the talented individuals who bring our favorite stories to life.
