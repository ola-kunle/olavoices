Kristen Bell Blindsided by Podcast Announcement

Fox News uses 15-year-old audio without her knowledge

Just Jared
November 21, 2025
3 min read

Imagine waking up one morning to find your voice being used in a project you knew nothing about. This is exactly what happened to actress Kristen Bell, who was recently blindsided by the announcement of a new podcast on Fox News Media's Fox Faith vertical. According to Just Jared, Bell will be heard as the voice of Mary Magdalene in the 'Life of Jesus Podcast', but she had no clue the project was being produced.

The news has sent shockwaves through the entertainment industry, with many questioning the ethics of using audio recorded 15 years ago without the actress's consent. A rep for Bell has revealed that the project is indeed using old recordings, leaving many to wonder how this could have happened.

For those unfamiliar with the project, the 'Life of Jesus Podcast' is a new series launching on Fox News Media's Fox Faith vertical. The podcast aims to tell the story of Jesus, with various voice actors bringing the characters to life. However, the use of Bell's audio without her knowledge has raised more questions than answers.

The voice-over industry is also taking notice of this development, as it highlights the importance of consent and transparency in voice-over work. Voice actors and content creators are now more aware than ever of the need to protect their intellectual property and ensure that their work is used in a way that aligns with their values. This incident has sparked a conversation about the rights of voice-over artists and the need for clearer contracts and agreements.

The impact of this news extends beyond the entertainment industry, as it touches on issues of consent and control. In an era where audio and video content can be easily manipulated and distributed, it's more important than ever for creators to have a say in how their work is used. This incident serves as a reminder of the need for greater transparency and accountability in the use of audio and video content.

As the 'Life of Jesus Podcast' prepares to launch, many are left wondering what this means for the future of voice-over work. Will this incident lead to greater protections for voice actors, or will it become a precedent for the use of old recordings without consent? Only time will tell, but one thing is certain - this news has sparked a vital conversation about the rights and responsibilities of voice-over artists.

In conclusion, the announcement of the 'Life of Jesus Podcast' has left Kristen Bell and many others feeling blindsided and concerned about the use of their audio. As the voice-over industry continues to evolve, it's essential that we prioritize transparency, consent, and accountability. The use of old recordings without consent is a wake-up call for voice-over artists and content creators, reminding us of the need to protect our intellectual property and ensure that our work is used in a way that aligns with our values.
