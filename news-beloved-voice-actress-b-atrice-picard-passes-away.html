Beloved Voice Actress Béatrice Picard Passes Away

French-Canadian Dub of Marge Simpson Lost at 96

TheWrap
December 12, 2025
4 min read

Imagine a world without the soothing voice of Marge Simpson, the iconic matriarch of the beloved animated series 'The Simpsons.' For many French-Canadian fans, that world has become a harsh reality with the passing of Béatrice Picard, the talented voice actress who brought Marge to life in the Quebec French dub for an impressive 33 years. According to TheWrap, Picard died on Tuesday at the remarkable age of 96, leaving behind a legacy that will be deeply felt by fans and voice actors alike.

Béatrice Picard's remarkable career spanned multiple decades, with her work on 'The Simpsons' being one of her most notable and enduring roles. As the voice of Marge, she became an integral part of many French-Canadian families' lives, providing a sense of comfort and familiarity with her warm and nurturing tone. Her dedication to her craft and her ability to bring depth and nuance to her characters have inspired countless voice actors and actresses around the world.

The news of Picard's passing has sent shockwaves through the voice-over community, with many professionals taking to social media to pay tribute to her remarkable talent and legacy. As a voice actress, Picard's work on 'The Simpsons' was not only a testament to her skill and versatility but also a reminder of the significant impact that voice actors can have on popular culture. The voice-over industry, in particular, has been shaped by talented individuals like Picard, who have helped to bring animated characters to life and have inspired new generations of voice actors to pursue their passions.

The voice-over angle of this story cannot be overstated. Picard's work on 'The Simpsons' has created opportunities for voice actors to explore the world of animation and dubbing, and her legacy will continue to inspire voice talent to push the boundaries of their craft. The demand for high-quality voice-over work is on the rise, with the growth of streaming services and the increasing popularity of animated content. As a result, voice actors are faced with new challenges and opportunities, from navigating the complexities of accents and dialects to developing their skills in areas such as script interpretation and character development. The passing of Béatrice Picard serves as a reminder of the importance of preserving and promoting the art of voice acting, and the need for voice actors to continue innovating and adapting to the evolving needs of the industry.

So why does the passing of Béatrice Picard matter? It matters because it reminds us of the power of voice to transcend borders and cultures, to bring people together, and to create a sense of community and shared experience. It matters because it highlights the significant contribution that voice actors make to our lives, often working behind the scenes to bring joy, comfort, and inspiration to audiences around the world. And it matters because it encourages us to appreciate the art of voice acting, to recognize the skill and craftsmanship that goes into creating memorable characters, and to support the talented individuals who dedicate their lives to this craft.

As we say goodbye to Béatrice Picard, we are reminded of the enduring legacy of her work and the impact that she has had on the world of voice acting. Her passing is a poignant reminder of the importance of preserving our cultural heritage, of celebrating the talented individuals who have shaped our lives, and of continuing to innovate and push the boundaries of the voice-over industry. As we look to the future, we honor Picard's memory by recognizing the power of voice to inspire, to educate, and to bring people together, and by supporting the next generation of voice actors who will carry on her legacy.
