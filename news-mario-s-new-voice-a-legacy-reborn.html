Mario's New Voice: A Legacy Reborn

Kevin Afghani steps into iconic role, praises predecessor

My Nintendo News
January 15, 2026
4 min read

Imagine being the voice behind a beloved character that has been a part of millions of childhoods worldwide. For Kevin Afghani, this is now a reality as he takes on the iconic role of Mario, a character previously voiced by the legendary Charles Martinet. According to My Nintendo News, in an exclusive interview with USA Today, Afghani shared his thoughts on stepping into Martinet's shoes, calling him 'amazing.' This transition marks a significant moment in the world of voice acting, where the passing of a torch from one voice actor to another can have a profound impact on the character's legacy and the industry as a whole.

The voice of Mario is more than just a sound; it's an emotional connection that gamers have shared for decades. Charles Martinet's voice has been synonymous with the character since the 1990s, bringing joy and excitement to fans of all ages. The decision to replace Martinet with Afghani has sparked both curiosity and concern among fans, who are eager to see how the new voice will impact their gaming experience. As Afghani navigates this new role, he will undoubtedly face the challenge of honoring Martinet's legacy while also bringing his own unique interpretation to the character.

The voice-over industry is a complex and multifaceted field, where talent, technique, and adaptability are essential for success. The shift in Mario's voice is a reminder that voice actors are not just providing a service but are also the guardians of a character's identity. This responsibility is especially significant in the gaming industry, where characters like Mario are often at the forefront of the gaming experience. As the industry continues to evolve, with advancements in technology and the rise of new platforms, the role of the voice actor will become even more critical in shaping the narrative and emotional resonance of games.

The impact of this change extends beyond the gaming community, touching on the broader voice-over industry. It highlights the importance of versatility and the ability of voice actors to adapt to new roles and characters. For aspiring voice-over professionals, Kevin Afghani's story serves as a testament to the opportunities that exist in the industry, where dedication and talent can lead to iconic roles. Moreover, it underscores the challenges that come with replacing a beloved voice, emphasizing the need for sensitivity and respect for the character's history and the fans' emotional investment.

As Kevin Afghani embarks on this new journey, the voice-over community will be watching with keen interest. His success will not only depend on his ability to bring a fresh perspective to the role but also on his capacity to honor the legacy that Charles Martinet has built. This transition is a poignant reminder of the power of voice acting to evoke emotions, create connections, and transcend generations. As the gaming and voice-over industries continue to intersect and influence each other, stories like Afghani's will become increasingly important, showcasing the human side of characters that have become an integral part of our shared cultural heritage.

In conclusion, the new voice of Mario is more than just a change in personnel; it's a chapter in the ongoing story of how voice acting shapes our entertainment experiences. As we look to the future, it's clear that the voice-over industry will play an ever-more vital role in crafting the narratives that captivate and inspire us. Whether you're a gamer, a voice-over professional, or simply someone who appreciates the magic of bringing characters to life, Kevin Afghani's journey as the new voice of Mario is a story that will resonate deeply, reminding us of the enduring power of voice to touch hearts and minds.
