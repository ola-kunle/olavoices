Felicia Day's Fallout Role: A Voice-Over Benchmark

Exploring the impact on voice-over industry and content creation

PC Gamer
December 7, 2025
3 min read

Imagine bringing a character to life with just your voice, only to have that character become an iconic figure in gaming culture. For Felicia Day, her role as Veronica Santangelo in Fallout: New Vegas was 'one of the best roles I've ever got to play,' as she expressed her gratitude to the game's developers. According to PC Gamer, Day's character has become a beloved figure in the Fallout universe, with fans eagerly awaiting her potential appearance in the upcoming Fallout TV series.

However, it was recently announced that Day will not be reprising her role in the show's second season, leaving many fans disappointed. The news has sparked a wave of discussion among gamers and voice-over professionals alike, highlighting the significance of voice acting in the gaming industry. The Fallout series, known for its engaging storylines and memorable characters, has been a benchmark for voice-over work in the gaming world.

The voice-over industry plays a vital role in bringing characters like Veronica Santangelo to life. Voice actors must possess a unique ability to convey emotion, personality, and depth through their voice alone. The success of a character like Santangelo is a testament to the power of voice-over work in creating immersive gaming experiences. As the gaming industry continues to evolve, the demand for skilled voice actors will only continue to grow, creating new opportunities for voice-over professionals to make their mark.

The impact of Day's character on the voice-over industry extends beyond the gaming world. Her success has inspired a new generation of voice actors, demonstrating the potential for voice-over work to transcend traditional acting roles. The fact that Day's character has become an iconic figure in gaming culture is a reminder of the significant role that voice actors play in shaping our emotional connections to the characters and stories we encounter.

As the Fallout TV series moves forward without Day's character, fans will be left to wonder what could have been. However, the legacy of Veronica Santangelo will live on, serving as a reminder of the enduring power of voice-over work in the gaming industry. The news of Day's departure from the series may be disappointing, but it also highlights the ever-changing landscape of the voice-over industry, where new opportunities and challenges emerge with each passing day.

In conclusion, Felicia Day's role in Fallout: New Vegas has set a high standard for voice-over work in the gaming industry. As the voice-over industry continues to evolve, it will be exciting to see how voice actors rise to the challenge of creating immersive characters and stories that captivate audiences worldwide. The connection between voice-over work and the gaming industry is a powerful one, and it will be fascinating to see how this relationship develops in the years to come.
