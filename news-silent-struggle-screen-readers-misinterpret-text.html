Silent Struggle: Screen Readers Misinterpret Text

Word-break property issue affects VoiceOver functionality

Wordpress.org
December 7, 2025
3 min read

Imagine being unable to hear the words that are meant to empower and inform you. For individuals who rely on screen readers, a recent discovery has shed light on a critical issue that affects their daily lives. According to Wordpress.org, a ticket was created to address the problem of the word-break property in screen reader only CSS, which can cause VoiceOver to read text strangely.

The issue was noticed while using the screen-reader-only class from WordPress, where the word-break property set to a value other than normal can cause VoiceOver to read text in an unusual manner. In one example, the phrase 'read text' was read as 'R E A D' followed by a pause, highlighting the confusion this can cause for users who rely on screen readers. This problem not only affects individuals with visual impairments but also has significant implications for the voice-over industry.

The voice-over industry, which provides the voice behind screen readers, is deeply connected to this issue. Voice actors and content creators must consider the accessibility of their work, ensuring that the text they provide can be read correctly by screen readers. This includes considering the word-break property and its potential impact on the clarity of the text. According to Wordpress.org, this issue can cause VoiceOver to misinterpret text, leading to confusion and frustration for users. As the demand for accessible content continues to grow, voice-over professionals must stay informed about these issues to provide high-quality services.

The emotional and social impact of this issue cannot be overstated. For individuals who rely on screen readers, the ability to access information is a fundamental right. When this access is compromised, it can lead to feelings of isolation and exclusion. The voice-over industry has a critical role to play in promoting accessibility and inclusivity, and staying informed about issues like the word-break property is essential. By doing so, voice-over professionals can help create a more inclusive and equitable environment for all users.

In conclusion, the issue of the word-break property in screen reader only CSS is a critical one that affects not only individuals with visual impairments but also the voice-over industry as a whole. As we move forward, it is essential that we prioritize accessibility and inclusivity, ensuring that all users can access the information they need. The voice-over industry has a unique opportunity to promote positive change and create a more accessible world, one word at a time.
