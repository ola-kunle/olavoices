The Challenge:

A US-based non-profit working in Sub-Saharan Africa needed explainer videos for their educational initiatives. Their previous campaign using a standard American voice had low engagement rates among their target African diaspora communities and received feedback about "cultural disconnect."

The Solution:

We delivered authentic Nigerian-English narration that maintained professional clarity while incorporating the natural warmth and rhythm that resonates with African and diaspora audiences. The voice bridged cultural authenticity with Western accessibility.

The Results:

  • Social media engagement jumped 45% across all platforms
  • Donor inquiries from African diaspora communities increased by 89%
  • The campaign was nominated for a diversity in marketing award

What These Results Teach Us

Across these diverse projects—non-profit, corporate, and commercial—several patterns emerge:

  • Authenticity drives engagement: Audiences can sense when a voice truly represents their cultural experience
  • Cultural intelligence scales: The same authentic approach works across different industries and campaign types
  • ROI is measurable: Diverse voice selection isn't just about inclusion—it delivers concrete business results
  • Trust accelerates action: When audiences trust the voice, they're more likely to complete courses, click links, and convert
