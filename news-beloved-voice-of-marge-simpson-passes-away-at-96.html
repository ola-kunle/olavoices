Beloved Voice of Marge Simpson Passes Away at 96

Montreal-born Béatrice Picard leaves lasting legacy

Cracked.com
December 12, 2025
3 min read

Imagine a world without the iconic voice of Marge Simpson - a world that is now a reality. The passing of Béatrice Picard, the French-Canadian voice of Marge, has left fans around the globe in mourning. According to Cracked.com, the 96-year-old Montreal-born actress had a distinguished career in movie, theater, and television. Her legacy extends far beyond the Simpsons, with a body of work that has touched the hearts of many.

Béatrice Picard's contribution to the world of voice acting is a testament to the power of talent and dedication. As a member of the Order of Canada, she was recognized for her outstanding achievements in the arts. Her work on the French-Canadian version of the Simpsons has been a staple of many childhoods, bringing joy and laughter to countless fans. The news of her passing has sparked an outpouring of tributes and condolences from fans and fellow actors alike.

The voice-over industry is a unique and often overlooked aspect of the entertainment world. Yet, it is the voices behind our favorite characters that bring them to life. Béatrice Picard's passing serves as a reminder of the importance of voice acting and the impact it has on our culture. For voice-over professionals, her legacy is a source of inspiration and a reminder of the opportunities and challenges that come with this line of work. The demand for talented voice actors continues to grow, with the rise of animation, video games, and audiobooks. However, it also presents challenges, such as the need for versatility, creativity, and adaptability. As the industry continues to evolve, it is essential for voice-over professionals to stay ahead of the curve and continue to push the boundaries of their craft.

The passing of Béatrice Picard is not just a loss for the voice-over industry; it is also a loss for the cultural landscape. Her voice has been a part of many people's lives, providing comfort, entertainment, and inspiration. The emotional and social impact of her work cannot be overstated. As we mourn the loss of this talented actress, we are reminded of the power of voice acting to bring people together and transcend borders. The French-Canadian version of the Simpsons, with Béatrice Picard's iconic voice, has been a bridge between cultures, allowing fans to connect with the beloved characters in their own language.

As we reflect on the life and legacy of Béatrice Picard, we are reminded of the significance of voice acting in our lives. Her passing is a poignant reminder of the importance of preserving our cultural heritage and celebrating the talented individuals who bring our favorite characters to life. The voice-over industry will continue to thrive, with new talent emerging and innovative technologies changing the landscape. However, it is essential to remember the pioneers like Béatrice Picard, who paved the way for future generations of voice actors. As we look to the future, we must honor her legacy by continuing to push the boundaries of voice acting and celebrating the art of bringing characters to life.
